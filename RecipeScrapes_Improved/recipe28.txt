So good you&#8217;ll be roasting two turkeys!</h2><h2><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-13030" src="http://www.thewickednoodle.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Turkey-Tetrazzini.jpg" alt="Turkey Tetrazzini with Parmesan Panko Topping - leftover Thanksgiving turkey works great or use chicken if that's what you've got. I have at least five more coming down the pipeline in the next week and a half, all easy and all delicious. But right now I want to talk turkey leftovers.</p><p>Turkey Tetrazzini is one of my very favorite ways to use up leftover Thanksgiving turkey. When leftovers can be transformed into an entirely new dish, I&#8217;m always on board. I love how customizable this recipe is, too &#8211; add mushrooms and peas if you like or simply leave them out. Leftover green beans? Toss &#8217;em in! No leftover turkey? Use chicken. It&#8217;s all good &#8211; <em>literally</em>.</p><p><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-13031" src="http://www.thewickednoodle.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Turkey-Tetrazzini-Parmesan-Panko-Crust.jpg" alt="Turkey Tetrazzini with Parmesan Panko Topping - leftover Thanksgiving turkey works great or use chicken if that's what you've got. The other day I was busy (and therefore absentminded) and forgot to add it. I was thinking, &#8220;hmmmm&#8230;what&#8217;s missing from this?&#8221; when I remembered I&#8217;d forgotten the extra seasoning. It&#8217;s amazing what a difference the freshly ground black pepper makes! The leftovers reheat well, too (yes, you&#8217;ll definitely have leftovers from your leftovers :) It makes a LOT. Pour mixture into a greased 2-quart casserole dish or 13x9 pan.</li><li id="mpprecipe-instruction-2" class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Melt butter in a microwave-safe bowl.
