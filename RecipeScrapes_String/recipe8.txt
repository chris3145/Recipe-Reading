Like any food, too much of a good thing is, for lack of more technical nutritional jargon, <em>bad</em>. So I do try to switch up my oils from day to day, usually between olive, <a href="http://amzn.to/29yOj0K">coconut</a>, <a href="http://amzn.to/29TymRg">sunflower</a>, <a href="http://amzn.to/29AbrZl">flax seed</a>, and <a href="http://amzn.to/29TyEaM">canola</a>.</p><p>But olive oil will always have my heart. Growing up, my mother had at least 5 large bottles of it on hand, and I would watch her blow through them in a matter of weeks. There’s a story in my cookbook about the way she would spoon-feed herself frozen peas in a bath of olive oil so deep, from far away, you’d think she was eating soup. Despite these questionable habits, my mother remains to this day, the most health-conscious woman I know. And she’s always ten years ahead of a trend, if her pre-millennial love of millet is any indication.</p><p>If there’s one fad that her life-long love of olive oil fits under it’s the newly coined “Mediterranean Diet”—otherwise known throughout eternity as the way Greek people eat. When I explore new cuisines, I love generically asking at restaurants for a side of “sauce” to see what they bring. In Greece, when I did this, what I got was a sauceboat of pure chartreuse-tinged <a href="http://amzn.to/2adLa3H">olive oil</a>. I’m just adhering to the trends, people.<span id="more-7837"></span></p><p>One of my favorite dishes from my recent trip was I<span class="_GAS2">mam Bayildi</span> from <a href="http://feedmephoebe.com/2014/07/hungry-girls-guide-hydra-greece/">a little Taverna in Hydra</a>. The pan-fried eggplant casserole is technically a Turkish dish, but I saw it on plenty of menus throughout Greece. It&#8217;s baked with tomatoes, garlic, and herbs and served room temperature in a lasagna-like slab. One slice is so decadent and delicious in its simplicity, you might almost forget about your old friend from across the Aegean, <a href="http://feedmephoebe.com/2013/10/baked-eggplant-parmesan-penne/">Eggplant Parmesan</a>. zlclear"><div class="fl-l width-50"><p id="zlrecipe-prep-time">Prep Time: <span itemprop="prepTime" content="PT20M">20 minutes</span></p><p id="zlrecipe-cook-time">Cook Time: <span itemprop="cookTime" src="http://feedmephoebe.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/IMG_1939.jpg" title="Turkish Eggplant Casserole with Tomatoes (Imam Bayildi)" alt="Turkish Eggplant Casserole with Tomatoes (Imam Bayildi)"  /></p><div id="zlrecipe-summary" itemprop="description"><p class="summary italic">This Turkish eggplant recipe "Imam Bayildi" is a perfect vegetarian weeknight dinner. It's great for making ahead of time, as the flavors only get better.  Inspired by <a href="http://greekvegetarian.blogspot.com/2013/07/imam-bayildi-limnian-style-eggplant-stew.html" class="summary-link" target="_blank">The Greek Vegetarian</a>.</p></div></div><p id="zlrecipe-ingredients" class="h-4 strong">Ingredients</p><ul id="zlrecipe-ingredients-list"><li id="zlrecipe-ingredient-0" class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">2 medium eggplant, thinly sliced (1/4 inch thick)</li><li id="zlrecipe-ingredient-1" class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">Sea salt</li><li id="zlrecipe-ingredient-2" class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">Olive oil</li><li id="zlrecipe-ingredient-3" class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">1 small yellow onion, diced</li><li id="zlrecipe-ingredient-4" class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">2 large garlic cloves</li><li id="zlrecipe-ingredient-5" class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">1/4 teaspoon red chili flakes</li><li id="zlrecipe-ingredient-6" class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">Dash of cinnamon</li><li id="zlrecipe-ingredient-7" class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">One 14.5 ounce can diced tomatoes (I like <a href="http://amzn.to/29GH0W2" class="ingredient-link" target="_blank">this</a> brand)</li><li id="zlrecipe-ingredient-8" class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">2 tablespoons finely chopped fresh parsley, divided</li></ul><p id="zlrecipe-instructions" class="h-4 strong">Instructions</p><ol id="zlrecipe-instructions-list" class="instructions"><li id="zlrecipe-instruction-0" class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F.</li><li id="zlrecipe-instruction-1" class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Lightly salt the eggplant slices and allow to stand for 20 minutes. Pat the slices dry and set aside.</li><li id="zlrecipe-instruction-2" class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Heat a thin layer of olive oil in a large oven proof (preferably <a href="http://amzn.to/29GGY0w" class="instruction-link" target="_blank">cast iron</a>) skillet. Pan fry the eggplant in batches over medium-high heat until golden brown on both sides, about 2 minutes per side. Add more oil as necessary between batches so the eggplant doesn't burn, but try not to have too heavy a hand - the pan just needs to be greased. Remove the eggplant to a plate.</li><li id="zlrecipe-instruction-3" class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Add the onion to the skillet and saute over medium heat until soft, 5 minutes. Add the garlic, chili flakes, 1/2 teaspoon sea salt and cinnamon. Cook for one minute more, until fragrant. Carefully pour in the tomatoes and simmer until thickened slightly, 5 minutes. Remove from the heat and stir in half the parsley.</li><li id="zlrecipe-instruction-4" class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">If using an ovenproof skillet, remove 3/4 of the sauce to a bowl, leaving a thin layer behind. Otherwise, add 1/4 cup of sauce to the bottom of a 8x8 or comparable <a href="http://amzn.to/29BQI6q" class="instruction-link" target="_blank">casserole dish</a>. Arrange one layer of eggplant evenly in the bottom of the skillet or dish. Slather with a layer of sauce, and repeat with the remaining eggplant and sauce - like you would a lasagna.</li><li id="zlrecipe-instruction-5" class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Cover with foil and bake in the oven for 45 minutes, until the eggplant is very soft and the sauce has reduced. Allow to sit for 10 minutes before cutting into it. Garnish with the remaining parsley and serve warm or at room temperature.</li></ol><p id="zlrecipe-notes" class="h-4 strong">Notes</p><div id="zlrecipe-notes-list"><p class="notes">I streamlined this recipe by using an oven proof skillet to fry the eggplant, cook the sauce, and then make the casserole.
