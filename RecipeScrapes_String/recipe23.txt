			Travelling Noodles &#8211; Soup Up Your Packed Lunch!		</h1>

					<p>
						<p>These are my New Years Resolution lunch pots.</p>
<p>They&#8217;re quick, easy and perfect for those who want to cut back on the unhealthy stuff, cut back on spending but don&#8217;t want to sacrifice their enjoyment&#8230; especially come lunch time!</p>
<p>Throw a pot together in the evening, seal it up, pop it into the fridge and take it with you in the morning. Then all you&#8217;ll need is a little hot water to bring your spicy, fragrant, healthy noodle soup to life.</p>
<p>Scoop, spoon &#8216;n&#8217; slurp to your hearts content!</p>
<p><span id="more-22813"></span></p>
<p><img class="alignnone wp-image-22833 size-full" src="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-20.jpg" alt="homemade pot noodle-20" width="652" height="1000" srcset="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-20.jpg 652w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-20-196x300.jpg 196w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-20-626x960.jpg 626w" sizes="(max-width: 652px) 100vw, 652px" /></p>
<p><img class="alignnone wp-image-22831 size-full" src="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-18.jpg" alt="homemade pot noodle-18" width="663" height="1000" srcset="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-18.jpg 663w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-18-199x300.jpg 199w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-18-636x960.jpg 636w" sizes="(max-width: 663px) 100vw, 663px" /></p>
<p>Warm, comforting, filling and you can use whatever you&#8217;ve got in the cupboards&#8230; so you&#8217;ll never get bored!</p>
<p>The idea is similar to HFW&#8217;s Veggie Pot Noodles from his <a href="http://www.amazon.co.uk/gp/product/1607744724?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=3194&amp;creative=21330&amp;creativeASIN=1607744724&amp;linkCode=shr&amp;tag=thelond-21&amp;linkId=552QH22PTSQT2CLP" target="_blank">Veggie Cookbook</a> (which is a corker of a book!), but the recipe is quite different so here&#8217;s what you&#8217;ll need for 1 pot:</p>
<p>A pot with a lid (Pyrex ones are safest but <a href="http://www.amazon.co.uk/gp/product/B007VP8HD4?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=3194&amp;creative=21330&amp;creativeASIN=B007VP8HD4&amp;linkCode=shr&amp;tag=thelond-21&amp;linkId=IZFH6CR2QVUAEBJG&amp;qid=1420240027&amp;sr=8-3&amp;keywords=kilner+jars" target="_blank">I use these</a>)</p>
<p>1 handful of dried noodles (try <a href="http://bit.ly/1IvJtqT" target="_blank">zero cal noodles</a> if you want to be <em>really</em> good!)</p>
<p>1 handful of mixed asian veg</p>
<p>1 handful of left-over cooked chicken / cooked prawns / tofu</p>
<p>Half handful of bean sprouts</p>
<p>1 spring onion</p>
<p>1tsp fresh or dried coriander</p>
<p>1tsp hot sauce (I like Sriacha)</p>
<p>1tsp miso paste</p>
<p>1tsp bouillon/stock powder (make sure it has no MSG)</p>
<p>1tsp tom yum paste (optional)</p>
<p>1tbsp soy sauce</p>
<p>(Remember, you&#8217;re welcome to throw in whatever you&#8217;ve got to hand; there are unlimited possible combos for these babies!)</p>
<p><img class="alignnone wp-image-22814 size-full" src="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-1.jpg" alt="homemade pot noodle-1" width="1000" height="759" srcset="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-1.jpg 1000w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-1-300x228.jpg 300w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-1-960x729.jpg 960w" sizes="(max-width: 1000px) 100vw, 1000px" /></p>
<p>Cook your noodles according to the package.</p>
<p>When they&#8217;re done, pop them into the bottom of your pot.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone wp-image-22815 size-full" src="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-2.jpg" alt="homemade pot noodle-2" width="661" height="1000" srcset="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-2.jpg 661w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-2-198x300.jpg 198w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-2-635x960.jpg 635w" sizes="(max-width: 661px) 100vw, 661px" /></p>
<p>Flatten them in and make sure they&#8217;re nicely spread out.</p>
<p>Leave to cool.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone wp-image-22816 size-full" src="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-3.jpg" alt="homemade pot noodle-3" width="668" height="1000" srcset="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-3.jpg 668w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-3-200x300.jpg 200w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-3-641x960.jpg 641w" sizes="(max-width: 668px) 100vw, 668px" /></p>
<p>While the noodles cool, make your taste paste.</p>
<p>In a small bowl mix together your miso, bouillon, soy, tom yum paste, as much hot sauce as you fancy. Even if you don&#8217;t like spicy stuff, just add a little. It gives the soup a great bite and boots your metabolism up a gear. Great if you get a New Year&#8217;s cold, too!</p>
<p>When your pots are nice and chilled, add your sauce.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone wp-image-22817 size-full" src="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-4.jpg" alt="homemade pot noodle-4" width="667" height="1000" srcset="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-4.jpg 667w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-4-200x300.jpg 200w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-4-640x960.jpg 640w" sizes="(max-width: 667px) 100vw, 667px" /></p>
<p><img class="alignnone wp-image-22818 size-full" src="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-5.jpg" alt="homemade pot noodle-5" width="667" height="1000" srcset="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-5.jpg 667w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-5-200x300.jpg 200w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-5-640x960.jpg 640w" sizes="(max-width: 667px) 100vw, 667px" /></p>
<p>Pile in your chosen meat. Use whatever you fancy, but make sure it&#8217;s cooked.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone wp-image-22819 size-full" src="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-6.jpg" alt="homemade pot noodle-6" width="667" height="1000" srcset="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-6.jpg 667w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-6-200x300.jpg 200w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-6-640x960.jpg 640w" sizes="(max-width: 667px) 100vw, 667px" /></p>
<p>Pack in a handful of sliced veg and bean sprouts.</p>
<p>Bean sprouts are heroes in the nutrition world! Bursting with vit C, B vitamins and protein. So be generous!</p>
<p><img class="alignnone wp-image-22820 size-full" src="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-7.jpg" alt="homemade pot noodle-7" width="667" height="1000" srcset="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-7.jpg 667w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-7-200x300.jpg 200w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-7-640x960.jpg 640w" sizes="(max-width: 667px) 100vw, 667px" /></p>
<p>Top with sliced spring onion.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone wp-image-22821 size-full" src="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-8.jpg" alt="homemade pot noodle-8" width="667" height="1000" srcset="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-8.jpg 667w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-8-200x300.jpg 200w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-8-640x960.jpg 640w" sizes="(max-width: 667px) 100vw, 667px" /></p>
<p>Sprinkle in your coriander, I didn&#8217;t have any fresh so used dried.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone wp-image-22822 size-full" src="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-9.jpg" alt="homemade pot noodle-9" width="667" height="1000" srcset="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-9.jpg 667w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-9-200x300.jpg 200w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-9-640x960.jpg 640w" sizes="(max-width: 667px) 100vw, 667px" /></p>
<p>And you&#8217;re done!</p>
<p><img class="alignnone wp-image-22824 size-full" src="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-11.jpg" alt="homemade pot noodle-11" width="657" height="1000" srcset="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-11.jpg 657w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-11-197x300.jpg 197w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-11-631x960.jpg 631w" sizes="(max-width: 657px) 100vw, 657px" /></p>
<p>Seal it up and store in the fridge.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone wp-image-22825 size-full" src="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-12.jpg" alt="homemade pot noodle-12" width="1000" height="650" srcset="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-12.jpg 1000w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-12-300x195.jpg 300w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-12-960x624.jpg 960w" sizes="(max-width: 1000px) 100vw, 1000px" /></p>
<p>They keep for a few days, so you could always make a batch of them for the week ahead.</p>
<p>When you&#8217;re ready to chow, boil the kettle and pop open your pot.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone wp-image-22826 size-full" src="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-13.jpg" alt="homemade pot noodle-13" width="703" height="1000" srcset="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-13.jpg 703w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-13-211x300.jpg 211w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-13-675x960.jpg 675w" sizes="(max-width: 703px) 100vw, 703px" /></p>
<p>Pour boiling water in, until the top veggies are swimming.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone wp-image-22827 size-full" src="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-14.jpg" alt="homemade pot noodle-14" width="649" height="1000" srcset="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-14.jpg 649w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-14-195x300.jpg 195w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-14-623x960.jpg 623w" sizes="(max-width: 649px) 100vw, 649px" /></p>
<p><img class="alignnone wp-image-22828 size-full" src="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-15.jpg" alt="homemade pot noodle-15" width="667" height="1000" srcset="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-15.jpg 667w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-15-200x300.jpg 200w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-15-640x960.jpg 640w" sizes="(max-width: 667px) 100vw, 667px" /></p>
<p>Seal the pot and leave it for a couple of minutes.</p>
<p>If you&#8217;re like me you&#8217;ll love watching the water turn that sauce you made into the most delicious, deeply red soup!</p>
<p>When you can&#8217;t wait anymore, mix it up and tuck in.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone wp-image-22829 size-full" src="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-16.jpg" alt="homemade pot noodle-16" width="667" height="1000" srcset="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-16.jpg 667w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-16-200x300.jpg 200w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-16-640x960.jpg 640w" sizes="(max-width: 667px) 100vw, 667px" /></p>
<p><img class="alignnone wp-image-22830 size-full" src="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-17.jpg" alt="homemade pot noodle-17" width="654" height="1000" srcset="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-17.jpg 654w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-17-196x300.jpg 196w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-17-628x960.jpg 628w" sizes="(max-width: 654px) 100vw, 654px" /></p>
<p><img class="alignnone wp-image-22832 size-full" src="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-19.jpg" alt="homemade pot noodle-19" width="667" height="1000" srcset="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-19.jpg 667w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-19-200x300.jpg 200w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-19-640x960.jpg 640w" sizes="(max-width: 667px) 100vw, 667px" /></p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-22831" src="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-18.jpg" alt="homemade pot noodle-18" width="663" height="1000" srcset="http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-18.jpg 663w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-18-199x300.jpg 199w, http://www.thelondoner.me/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/homemade-pot-noodle-18-636x960.jpg 636w" sizes="(max-width: 663px) 100vw, 663px" /></p>

<p>You&#8217;ll never look at a supermarket sandwich the same way again!</p>
