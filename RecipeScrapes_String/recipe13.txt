<p class="entry-meta"><time class="entry-time" itemprop="datePublished" datetime="2014-10-19T10:45:59+00:00">October 19, 2014</time> By <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Person"><a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/author/jleung/" class="entry-author-link" itemprop="url" rel="author"><span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">Judy</span></a></span> <span class="entry-comments-link"><a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/2014/10/lanzhou-beef-noodle-soup/#comments">133 Comments</a></span> </p></header><a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/2014/10/lanzhou-beef-noodle-soup/" title="Lanzhou Beef Noodle Soup"><img width="600" height="900" src="http://thewoksoflife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/lanzhou-beef-noodle-soup-6.jpg" class="post-image" alt="Lanzhou Beef Noodle Soup, by thewoksoflife.com" srcset="http://thewoksoflife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/lanzhou-beef-noodle-soup-6.jpg 600w, http://thewoksoflife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/lanzhou-beef-noodle-soup-6-200x300.jpg 200w" sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px"/></a><div class="entry-content" itemprop="text"><div class="pin-it-btn-wrapper"><a href="//www.pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fthewoksoflife.com%2F2014%2F10%2Flanzhou-beef-noodle-soup%2F&media=http%3A%2F%2Fthewoksoflife.com%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2014%2F10%2Flanzhou-beef-noodle-soup-4.jpg&description=Lanzhou%20Beef%20Noodle%20Soup%2C%20by%20thewoksoflife.com" data-pin-do="buttonBookmark" data-pin-config="beside" data-pin-color="gray" rel="nobox"><img src="//assets.pinterest.com/images/pidgets/pin_it_button.png" title="Pin It" data-pib-button="true"/></a></div><p>It&#8217;s obvious that China has no shortage of noodle dishes and Chinese noodle soup recipes. More than you&#8217;ve probably ever heard or dreamt of. The variety is as expansive as China&#8217;s geography, and EVERYTHING is delicious. But among the many noodle dishes (and noodle soups in particular), one stands out.</p>
<p>Consisting of a flavorful, clear broth, shaved beef, tender Chinese radish slices, lots of cilantro and scallion, deep red chili oil, and chewy handmade noodles, Lanzhou Beef Noodle Soup (兰州拉面, <em>lanzhou lamian</em>) has the majority vote among 1.4 billion <em>very</em> culinarily-conscious citizens.</p>
<p>This fact has been confirmed by a quick search on China&#8217;s Google, baidu.com. Translating to Lanzhou Hand-Pulled Noodle Soup, this bowl of hot, spicy delight is deceptively simple and has spawned noodle chains across Beijing. In fact, we have a Lanzhou Beef Noodle Soup place right downstairs, about 100 yards from our apartment. My niece Kim came to visit us in Beijing this summer, and after many fancy restaurants and lavish meals, she declared this bowl of noodle soup to be the best thing she had in China. And it only costs $3! We made sure to feed her an extra bowl before leaving for the airport.</p>
<p>If you&#8217;ve been on The Woks of Life for a while, you&#8217;ve probably learned a thing or two about me from my recipes. I&#8217;m not a fussy person, and I love keeping things simple. This recipe is adapted from many conversations with the cooks and staff at our local noodle shop, and after much thought and experimentation, I&#8217;ve simplified things for the home cook. We&#8217;re not going to make noodles from scratch, so don&#8217;t worry. You can&#8217;t compromise flavor on the broth however, which is what brings the dish together.</p>
<p><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6883" src="http://thewoksoflife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/lanzhou-beef-noodle-soup-4.jpg" alt="Lanzhou Beef Noodle Soup, by thewoksoflife.com" width="600" height="847" srcset="http://thewoksoflife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/lanzhou-beef-noodle-soup-4.jpg 600w, http://thewoksoflife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/lanzhou-beef-noodle-soup-4-212x300.jpg 212w" sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px"/></p>
<p>You&#8217;ll need:</p>
<ul>
<li>4 lbs beef or pork soup bones</li>
<li>2 1/4 lbs (1 kg) beef shank</li>
<li>½ of a whole roasting chicken (or a leftover roasted chicken carcass)</li>
<li>10 cups water</li>
<li>4 cups (about 1 liter) <a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/2016/01/homemade-chicken-stock/" target="_blank">chicken stock</a></li>
<li>Spice mix (see recipe below)</li>
<li>Salt, to taste (I added about a tablespoon)</li>
<li>1/2 of a small <a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/asian-vegetables-beans-melons/#chinese-radish" target="_blank">Chinese radish</a>, quartered and thinly sliced</li>
<li>1 lb fresh or <a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/chinese-noodles-wrappers/#fresh-white-noodles" target="_blank">fresh or dried white noodles</a> (they can really be any thickness, as long as they are wheat-based noodles rather than rice noodles, and have a nice chew when cooked)</li>
<li>Hot chili oil, to taste (see recipe below)</li>
<li>chopped <a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/chinese-chives-scallions-aromatics-peppers/" target="_blank">scallion</a></li>
<li>chopped <a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/chinese-chives-scallions-aromatics-peppers/#cilantro" target="_blank">cilantro</a></li>
</ul>
<p>For the spice mix:</p>
<ul>
<li>7 <a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/chinese-spices-condiments/#star-anise" target="_blank">star anise</a></li>
<li>12 <a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/chinese-spices-condiments/#cloves" target="_blank">cloves</a></li>
<li>1 <a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/chinese-spices-condiments/#gui-pi" target="_blank">cinnamon stick</a></li>
<li>5 <a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/chinese-spices-condiments/#bay-leaf" target="_blank">bay leaves</a></li>
<li>6 large slices of <a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/chinese-spices-condiments/#dried-sand-ginger" target="_blank">sand ginger</a></li>
<li>1 1/2 teaspoons <a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/chinese-spices-condiments/#fennel-seed" target="_blank">fennel seeds</a></li>
<li>1 teaspoon <a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/chinese-spices-condiments/#cumin-seeds" target="_blank">cumin seeds</a></li>
<li>2 teaspoons <a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/chinese-spices-condiments/#sichuan-peppercorn" target="_blank">Sichuan peppercorns</a></li>
<li>1 1/2 teaspoons <a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/chinese-spices-condiments/#white-pepper" target="_blank">white peppercorns</a></li>
<li>5 licorice root slices (if you can&#8217;t find these, you can leave them out)</li>
<li>3 pieces <a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/chinese-spices-condiments/#dried-tangerine-peel" target="_blank">dried orange peel</a></li>
<li>1 <a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/chinese-spices-condiments/#black-cardamom" target="_blank">black cardamom</a></li>
</ul>
<p>Check our our <a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/chinese-spices-condiments/" target="_blank">Chinese Dry spices and condiments page</a> to see more detailed descriptions and pictures of these and other  spices we use in our recipes.</p>
<p>For the hot chili oil:</p>
<ul>
<li>3/4 cup oil</li>
<li>2 <a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/chinese-spices-condiments/#star-anise" target="_blank">star anise</a></li>
<li>1/2 of a small <a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/chinese-spices-condiments/#gui-pi" target="_blank">cinnamon stick</a></li>
<li>½ teaspoon <a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/chinese-spices-condiments/#sichuan-peppercorn" target="_blank">Sichuan peppercorns</a></li>
<li>3 tablespoons <a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/chinese-spices-condiments/#red-chili-flakes" target="_blank">crushed red pepper flakes</a> (just the regular kind you&#8217;d sprinkle on your pizza)</li>
<li>1 teaspoon salt</li>
<li>1/4 teaspoon sugar</li>
</ul>
<p>Rinse the soup bones and pat dry. Roast them on a <a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/how-to/chinese-cooking-tools/#label-sheet-pan" target="_blank">baking sheet</a> at 400 degrees for 45 minutes. Bring a large pot of water to a boil, and add the beef shank and the chicken (or chicken carcass) to the pot. Bring everything to a boil again. Once boiling, remove the shank and the chicken, discard the water, and clean the pot. This process gets rid of any impurities, and will give your broth a cleaner flavor.</p>
<p>Put the beef shank and chicken back into the pot along with the roasted bones, 10 more cups of water, and 4 cups chicken stock. Make the spice mix by combining all ingredients and tying them tightly in cheese cloth with a bit of kitchen string. Add this to the pot as well and season with salt. Bring everything to a boil.</p>
<p>Once boiling, turn down the heat to low and let everything simmer for about 2 hours. After 2 hours have elapsed, remove the beef shank and set aside. Add the sliced radish and continue simmering for another hour. After that, use tongs to pick out and discard the spice pouch, chicken, and soup bones. Taste the broth for salt and adjust the seasoning if needed. The soup base is ready.</p>
<p>While all that is happening, you can take the time to make your chili oil. In a small pot, add the oil, star anise, cinnamon, and <a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/chinese-spices-condiments/#sichuan-peppercorn" target="_blank">Sichuan peppercorns.</a></p>
<p><a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/chinese-spices-condiments/" target="_blank"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7053" src="http://thewoksoflife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/chili-oil-spices.jpg" alt="Lanzhou Beef Noodle Soup, by thewoksoflife.com" width="600" height="902" srcset="http://thewoksoflife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/chili-oil-spices.jpg 600w, http://thewoksoflife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/chili-oil-spices-199x300.jpg 199w" sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px"/></a></p>
<p>Place the pot over very low heat and let everything toast together slowly for 15 minutes. Take care not to burn the spices. Use a slotted spoon to remove the spices, and turn off the heat. Let the oil cool for about 5 minutes, and then add the <a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/chinese-spices-condiments/#red-chili-flakes" target="_blank">chili flakes</a>. Slowly toast these in the hot oil until very fragrant (it should almost smell like popcorn), and very red. Stir in the salt and sugar.</p>
<p><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6880" src="http://thewoksoflife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/lanzhou-beef-noodle-soup-1.jpg" alt="Lanzhou Beef Noodle Soup, by thewoksoflife.com" width="600" height="760" srcset="http://thewoksoflife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/lanzhou-beef-noodle-soup-1.jpg 600w, http://thewoksoflife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/lanzhou-beef-noodle-soup-1-236x300.jpg 236w" sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px"/></p>
<p>Once the broth and chili oil are done, cook the noodles in a separate pot according to the package instructions. Divide the noodles among 6 bowls. Slice the cooled beef shank into thin slices, and fan them out over the noodles. To finish, add a big ladle of broth and radishes, a spoonful of hot chili oil, and a handful each (don&#8217;t be shy) of chopped <a href="http://thewoksoflife.com/chinese-chives-scallions-aromatics-peppers/" target="_blank">scallion and cilantro</a>.</p>
<p><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6881" src="http://thewoksoflife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/lanzhou-beef-noodle-soup-2.jpg" alt="Lanzhou Beef Noodle Soup, by thewoksoflife.com" width="600" height="888" srcset="http://thewoksoflife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/lanzhou-beef-noodle-soup-2.jpg 600w, http://thewoksoflife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/lanzhou-beef-noodle-soup-2-202x300.jpg 202w" sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px"/></p>
<p>You&#8217;re ready to enjoy this famous bowl of noodles like the best of them! Slurping is very much encouraged.</p>
<p><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6882" src="http://thewoksoflife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/lanzhou-beef-noodle-soup-3.jpg" alt="Lanzhou Beef Noodle Soup, by thewoksoflife.com" width="600" height="902" srcset="http://thewoksoflife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/lanzhou-beef-noodle-soup-3.jpg 600w, http://thewoksoflife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/lanzhou-beef-noodle-soup-3-199x300.jpg 199w" sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px"/><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6884" src="http://thewoksoflife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/lanzhou-beef-noodle-soup-5.jpg" alt="Lanzhou Beef Noodle Soup, by thewoksoflife.com" width="600" height="902" srcset="http://thewoksoflife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/lanzhou-beef-noodle-soup-5.jpg 600w, http://thewoksoflife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/lanzhou-beef-noodle-soup-5-199x300.jpg 199w" sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px"/></p>
