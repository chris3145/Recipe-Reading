class='at-above-post addthis_default_style addthis_toolbox at-wordpress-hide' data-title='Baby Broccoli Frittata with Feta' data-url='https://naturallyella.com/baby-broccoli-frittata-with-feta/'></div><p><img
class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-20898" src="https://32lxcujgg9-flywheel.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/09/Baby-Broccoli-Frittata-with-Feta-2.jpg" alt="Baby Broccoli Frittata with Feta | http://naturallyella.com" width="800" height="1200" data-jpibfi-post-excerpt="" data-jpibfi-post-url="https://naturallyella.com/baby-broccoli-frittata-with-feta/" data-jpibfi-post-title="Baby Broccoli Frittata with Feta" ><img
class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-20897" src="https://32lxcujgg9-flywheel.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/09/Baby-Broccoli-Frittata-with-Feta-1.jpg" alt="Baby Broccoli | http://naturallyella.com" width="800" height="1200" srcset="https://32lxcujgg9-flywheel.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/09/Baby-Broccoli-Frittata-with-Feta-1.jpg 800w, https://32lxcujgg9-flywheel.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/09/Baby-Broccoli-Frittata-with-Feta-1-225x337.jpg 225w" sizes="(max-width: 800px) 100vw, 800px" data-jpibfi-post-excerpt="" data-jpibfi-post-url="https://naturallyella.com/baby-broccoli-frittata-with-feta/" data-jpibfi-post-title="Baby Broccoli Frittata with Feta" ></p><p>Baby broccoli (also known as broccolini) is one of those items that grabs my attention at the store/market from time to time. It&#8217;s not broccoli but rather a hybrid of broccoli and kai-lan (Chinese broccoli or Chinese Kale). The result is something that looks like broccoli but is more tender and has a slightly more milder and sweeter flavor. I also love that it doesn&#8217;t take much cooking for the broccolini to really shine and this broccoli frittata is one of the best examples.</p><p><span
id="more-20896"></span></p><div
class="ne_pairings"><h2 class="center">Pairings for broccoli frittata</h2><p>I am of the mindset a broccoli frittata is a great meal for any time of the day. While it might seem better suited for breakfast, I can easily fit the frittata into lunch or even a light dinner. It takes a bit of time to cook, but nothing extreme and is mostly hands-off. If feta isn&#8217;t your favorite, use your favorite. I&#8217;ve been known to use smoked gouda or even cheddar.</p><p><strong>Breakfast</strong></p><p><em>Side</em>: Toss fresh berries with a bit of lemon juice and fresh mint. Since the frittata isn&#8217;t heavy on the herbs, the side can balance that out.</p><p><em>Drink</em>: Pick a lighter roast coffee and one that has hints of citrus to match the lemon in the berries.</p><p><strong>Lunch/Dinner</strong></p><p>Salad: A simple arugula salad is usually my go-to. I love this <a
href="http://naturallyella.com/arugula-lentil-salad/">simple lentil version</a> (or there&#8217;s always <a
href="http://naturallyella.com/warm-potato-arugula-salad/">this potato version</a> too.)</p><p>Drink: I&#8217;ve been mixing it up and while I&#8217;m still all about white wine, I&#8217;ve been loving Dry Chenin Blanc (particularly <a
href="http://store.drycreekvineyard.com/2013_Dry_Chenin_Blanc" target="_blank">this one</a>).</p></div><div
class="row"><div
class="span9"><div

itemprop="image" href="https://32lxcujgg9-flywheel.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/09/Baby-Broccoli-Frittata-with-Feta-2.jpg"/><div

class="ERSClear"></div><div
class="ERSDetails"><div
class="ERSAuthor"> Author:&nbsp;<span
itemprop="author">Erin Alderson</span></div><div
class="ERSClear"></div><div
class="ERSTimes"><div
class="ERSHead"> Prep time:&nbsp; <time
itemprop="prepTime" datetime="PT15M">15 mins</time></div><div
class="ERSHead"> Cook time:&nbsp; <time
itemprop="cookTime" datetime="PT30M">30 mins</time></div><div
class="ERSHead"> Total time:&nbsp; <time
itemprop="totalTime" datetime="PT45M">45 mins</time></div></div><div
class="ERSClear"></div><div
class="ERSHead"> Serves:&nbsp;<span
itemprop="recipeYield">2 to 3</span></div><div
class="ERSClear">&nbsp;</div></div><div
class="ERSIngredients"><div
class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">Ingredients</div><ul><li
class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">1 tablespoon olive oil</li><li
class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">1/2 cup thinly sliced red onion</li><li
class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">4 to 5 stalks baby broccoli, ends trimmed</li><li
class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">6 large eggs</li><li
class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">1/3 cup whole milk</li><li
class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">1/2 teaspoon black pepper</li><li
class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">1/4 teaspoon salt</li><li
class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">1/2 ounce crumbled feta</li></ul><div
class="ERSClear"></div></div><div
class="ERSInstructions"><div
class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">Instructions</div><ol><li
class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Preheat oven to 425˚.</li><li
class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Heat an 8" oven-safe skillet over medium, add 1/2 tablespoon olive oil followed by the onions. Cook the onions, stirring often, until tender and beginning to brown, 6 to 8 minutes. Remove and set aside.</li><li
class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Add the remaining 1/2 tablespoon olive oil to the pan. Place the baby broccoli spears into the pan and cook until the green brightens and the ends are browning, turning occasionally, 4 to 5 minutes. Remove from pan.</li><li
class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Whisk together the eggs, milk, black pepper, and salt. Pour into the pan and cook for roughly 5 minutes, just long enough for the bottom to set. Lay the cooked baby broccoli on top of the eggs then sprinkle with onions and feta.</li><li
class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Transfer pan to the oven and bake until the frittata has puffed and top is beginning to brown, 15 to 18 minutes.</li></ol><div
