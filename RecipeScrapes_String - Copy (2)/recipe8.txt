<h2><span style="color: #993366;">Spaghetti Squash Recipe with Mushrooms and Parmesan</span></h2>
<p>Sometimes the flavors of a creamy mushroom risotto call out to me. There&#8217;s nothing better than a spoonful of super umami rice and mushrooms. Unfortunately, eating risotto usually involves ingesting mammoth levels of butter &#8211; and leave me feeling like I swallowed a bowling ball. So I embarked on a mission to capture the flavors of risotto in a vegetable dish. The flavors in this spaghetti squash recipe are savory and very earthy, almost like that much loved mushroom risotto. However, instead of creamy grains of rice in this <strong><a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0547516916/ref=as_li_tf_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=211189&amp;creative=373489&amp;creativeASIN=0547516916&amp;link_code=as3&amp;tag=nyfobl-20" target="_blank">rustic style preparation</a>, </strong>we&#8217;re substituting beautiful golden strands of squash which yield a satisfying crunch. Baking and scraping the <strong>spaghetti squash</strong> couldn&#8217;t have been easier! I&#8217;ll admit to previously (and laboriously) <del>hacking</del> cutting squash into pieces before throwing them on a <strong><a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000G0KJG4/ref=as_li_tf_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=211189&amp;creative=373489&amp;creativeASIN=B000G0KJG4&amp;link_code=as3&amp;tag=nyfobl-20" target="_blank">sheet tray</a></strong>, making the whole experience much more tiring than it ever needed to be. Well, never again! The secret is out; bake THEN cut your spaghetti squash to avoid slicing through the hard and waxy raw exterior!</p>
<div id="attachment_9860" style="width: 610px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img class="wp-image-9860 size-full" src="http://www.pickledplum.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/spaghetti-squash-ingredients-info.jpg" alt="spaghetti squash ingredients info" width="600" height="900" srcset="http://www.pickledplum.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/spaghetti-squash-ingredients-info.jpg 600w, http://www.pickledplum.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/spaghetti-squash-ingredients-info-200x300.jpg 200w, http://www.pickledplum.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/spaghetti-squash-ingredients-info-260x390.jpg 260w, http://www.pickledplum.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/spaghetti-squash-ingredients-info-160x240.jpg 160w" sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px"/><p class="wp-caption-text">There are some potent health benefits you get by combining spaghetti squash, mushrooms, garlic, parsley, thyme and Parmesan.</p></div>
<p>For a while there, a slew of <strong>spaghetti squash</strong> <strong>recipes</strong> made appearances on <a href="https://www.pinterest.com/pickledplumfood/" target="_blank">Pinterest</a> and on some of my favorite food blogs, looking beautiful and mouthwatering. I wanted to give it a shot but had never cooked with spaghetti squash before. It looked complicated and too elaborate. I was comfortable in my butternut squash zone and secretly believed other gourd family members wouldn&#8217;t taste as good or be as easy to prepare. But a couple of weeks ago, I saw a recipe on <a href="http://www.steamykitchen.com/11285-baked-spaghetti-squash-with-garlic-and-butter.html" target="_blank">Steamy Kitchen</a> with step by step photos on how to work with spaghetti squash. That was enough to convince me to give it a try (thank you Jaden!). I&#8217;ve adapted the recipe by adding a few more ingredients and omitting the butter (to keep it a little lighter.)</p>
<p><img class="alignnone wp-image-9861 size-full" src="http://www.pickledplum.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/spaghetti-squash.jpg" alt="how to bake spaghetti squash" width="600" height="896" srcset="http://www.pickledplum.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/spaghetti-squash.jpg 600w, http://www.pickledplum.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/spaghetti-squash-200x300.jpg 200w, http://www.pickledplum.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/spaghetti-squash-260x388.jpg 260w, http://www.pickledplum.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/spaghetti-squash-160x238.jpg 160w" sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px"/></p>
<p>Spaghetti squash may be very low in calories but it&#8217;s actually quite filling! Especially when prepared with meaty mushrooms and a good dose of Parmesan cheese. It&#8217;s a side dish that&#8217;s a little heavy so I would recommend serving your spaghetti squash &#8220;risotto&#8221; with a lighter dish. I&#8217;m certainly glad I&#8217;ve given <em>spaghetti squash recipe</em> a try since it&#8217;s absolutely delicious! The flavors are milder and not quite as sweet as butternut squash, which makes a great vehicle for ingredients that are more savory and umami based. It&#8217;s a wonderful addition to the menu!</p>
<p><img class="alignnone wp-image-9870 size-full" src="http://www.pickledplum.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/sliced-spaghetti-squash.jpg" alt="baked spaghetti squash" width="600" height="824" srcset="http://www.pickledplum.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/sliced-spaghetti-squash.jpg 600w, http://www.pickledplum.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/sliced-spaghetti-squash-218x300.jpg 218w, http://www.pickledplum.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/sliced-spaghetti-squash-260x357.jpg 260w, http://www.pickledplum.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/sliced-spaghetti-squash-160x219.jpg 160w" sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px"/><strong>Other yummy spaghetti squash recipes:</strong></p>
