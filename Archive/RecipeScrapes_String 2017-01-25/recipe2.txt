				<section class="article__content entry-content js-post-gallery">
					<p>In depth research and experimentation have led me to the crystalline clear realization that the best flavor on earth is that of crunchy Cheetos. Not the regular ones. They&#8217;re crap. Just the crunchies. The reason of course is pure science. It&#8217;s a chemical gestalt of cheese powder (or powdered cheese depending on who your talking to, and a bunch of other &#8230; stuff. Well I think we&#8217;ve cracked the code and truth be told, there are multiple secret ingredients including: nutritional yeast which is actually good for you and available at your local health food store; cheddar cheese powder, which is available from most reputable spice purveyors; trashy, green canister &#8220;parmesan&#8221; cheese, which is available at most gas stations; and last but not least buttermilk powder which you can find on the baking aisle of most megamarts.</p>
<p><span id="more-4077"></span></p>
<p>Be forewarned, the resulting mix is extremely habit forming. Placing it on popcorn only makes it better &#8230; I mean worse.</p>
    <div class="blog-yumprint-recipe blog-yumprint-medium-image   blog-yumprint-numbered-methods " yumprintrecipe="xQ7s" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">    <img class="blog-yumprint-google-image" src="http://1g3qt034qrei26p6x54fpg09.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/04/alton-brown-triple-cheese-popcorn_200.jpg" style="display:block;position:absolute;left:-10000px;top:-10000px;" itemprop="image" />		<div class="blog-yumprint-photo-top" style="background-image: url(http://1g3qt034qrei26p6x54fpg09.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/04/alton-brown-triple-cheese-popcorn_200.jpg)"></div>	<div class="blog-yumprint-recipe-title" itemprop="name">Triple Cheese Popcorn</div>	<div class="blog-yumprint-recipe-published" itemprop="datePublished">2016-03-26 01:13:00</div>		<img class="blog-yumprint-photo-top-large" src="http://1g3qt034qrei26p6x54fpg09.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/04/alton-brown-triple-cheese-popcorn_200.jpg" />    <div class="blog-yumprint-serves">Serves 4</div>	<div class="blog-yumprint-header">		<div class='blog-yumprint-stars-reviews' itemprop="aggregateRating" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/AggregateRating" color="#666666" highlightcolor="#808080" emptycolor="#bfbfbf" rating="0" count="0">

		</div>		<div class="blog-yumprint-method-section" yumprintsection="1">			<div class="blog-yumprint-subheader">Instructions</div>			<ol class="blog-yumprint-methods" itemprop="recipeInstructions">				<li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="0">Process the parmesan cheese-stuff, nutritional yeast, buttermilk powder and kosher salt in a food processor to a fine, granular powder.</li>				<li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="1">Place the oil and popcorn in a large, 6-quart, metal mixing bowl. Cover with heavy-duty aluminum foil and poke ten slits in the top with a knife.</li>				<li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="2">Place the bowl over medium heat and shake constantly using a pair of tongs to hold the bowl.  Continue shaking until the popcorn finishes popping, approximately 3 minutes.</li>				<li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="3">Remove the bowl from the heat and carefully remove the foil.</li>				<li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="4">Melt the butter in the microwave. Slowly drizzle over the popcorn, while spinning the bowl. Add the cheese mixture, re-cover with the aluminum foil and shake to coat the popcorn with the cheese mixture. Serve immediately.</li>			</ol>
		</div>		<div class="blog-yumprint-note-section" yumprintsection="2">			<div class="blog-yumprint-subheader">Notes</div>			<ol class='blog-yumprint-notes'>				<li class="blog-yumprint-note-item" yumprintitem="0">Although the butter does bring flavor to the party, it does contain water so in time it will suck some of the crispness out of your corn. Using olive oil will help to avoid this wilting.</li>			</ol>
